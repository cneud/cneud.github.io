Researcher, Project Manager and Library Hacker from Berlin, Germany.

My interests are Computer Vision, Natural Language Processing, Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence and their use in Digital Libraries and Digital Humanities.

You can also find me on twitter, mastodon, github, orcid, scholar, keybase, linkedin.


2014 ‚Äî now @ Staatsbibliothek zu Berlin - Preußischer Kulturbesitz

In Berlin I took over the role as Project Coordinator of Europeana Newspapers and while the project outputs were well received, the TEL service was soon after discontinued. Throughout DSI-2, DSI-3, DSI-4, I worked on relaunching a Europeana newspaper collection, and on its exploitation in projects like Rise of Literacy, a data pilot with EUDAT, as CLARIN corpora, by Digital Humanities in Oceanic Exchanges.

Meanwhile I also worked with newspapers in Dahlie, contributed to a master plan for newspaper digitisation, the establishment of the German Newspaper Portal, and the exploration of newspapers for historical network analysis in the SoNAR (IDH) project.

In the OCR-D project, I can apply my experience from IMPACT, but with the technology of today. Machine Learning & Artificial Intelligence have become another interest of mine, with QURATOR establishing a state-of-the-art set of tools, extended and applied in Mensch.Maschine.Kultur. And I helped start another SBB-LAB.


2009 ‚Äî 2014 @ Koninklijke Bibliotheek

I was given a warm welcome and a lot of responsibility but also support and trust when I moved to the Netherlands as the Technical Manager, Work Packager Leader and Executive Board Member of the IMPACT project. Looking back, the vast amount of experts and large scale of the project helped me grow a lot. Later I helped establish the Impact Centre of Competence in Digitisation, including as Work Packager Leader in the SUCCEED project, and partner in the Early Modern OCR Project (eMOP).

The KB entered into a digitisation partnership with Google, and scalability again became very important. One of our use cases in the Scalable Preservation Environments (SCAPE) project made it to HadoopSummit Europe, another won a grant for HPC cloud use from the BiG Grid project. Quality research and tools, a culture of experimentation and the emergence of the Digital Humanities led us to found the KBLAB.

Working door to door with Europeana, we maintained a close work relationship, which led to the Europeana Newspapers project, where I was a Work Package Leader before I joined Staatsbibliothek zu Berlin as the Project Coordinator.


2003 ‚Äî 2009 @ Bayerische Staatsbibliothek

My journey into digital libraries began with a student job in the Munich Digitization Center (MDZ). Here I first encountered robots scanning books, and used Linux to produce metadata with Emacs (it was 2003). Later I became responsible for OCR in digitisation projects like Wiener Slawistischer Almanach, Baltische biographische Lexika, Historischer Atlas von Bayern, Francia, Digitale Monumenta Germaniae Historica and others that are now part of the Bayerische Landesbibliothek Online.

At MDZ I got involved in many tasks in digitisation, such as the integration of structured data in Reichstagsprotokolle and Reichstagsabgeordnete, the creation of authority data in the GND for BMLO, or the long-term preservation of data in BABS. In the Digi20 project, I designed an automated OCR workflow for the MDZ.

When the BSB entered into a partnership with Google to digitise all copyright-free holdings, I saw a new scale of mass-digitisation. Then the IMPACT project offered an opportunity to push innovation in OCR for historical documents, and I joined the Koninklijke Bibliotheek to work as the Technical Manager of the IMPACT project.
